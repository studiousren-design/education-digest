# AI in Education Digest — 19 June 2026

**UK / Collaborative Doctoral Research** — A UK-wide survey finds that a quarter of institutions have no visibility into whether industry-embedded PhD candidates are using AI in their research, and only a small fraction of universities are actively developing guidelines to address the gap. The finding comes as collaborative doctoral programmes grow rapidly and as AI norms diverge sharply between academic supervisors and industry partners — a structural mismatch few institutions are currently equipped to resolve.

*For educators:* If a PhD student's industry partner encourages AI use for efficiency while their university supervisor has no guidance or awareness, who bears responsibility for research integrity — and how should joint supervision agreements be rewritten?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/news/academic-industry-phd-students-face-conflicting-priorities

---

**Global / University Rankings Administration** — Times Higher Education has deployed AI to process the more than 270,000 evidence documents submitted by 2,152 institutions for its Impact Rankings, using automated systems to flag potential integrity issues at a scale no human team could manage alone. Human experts retain final judgement on all flagged cases, positioning the exercise as a working model for AI-assisted quality assurance in high-stakes, large-volume educational assessment.

*For educators:* As AI becomes the first reader of institutional evidence in major rankings, how should universities adapt their documentation practices — and who should audit the AI's own criteria for what it flags as suspect?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/world-university-rankings/ai-improving-our-rankings-human-expertise-will-remain-vital

---

**Global / Higher Education Institutions** — A practice guide published by Times Higher Education argues that AI literacy must be embedded as a core institutional requirement rather than an optional add-on for science or technology departments, drawing on six years of curriculum integration experience to propose a three-part framework covering AI fundamentals, ethics, and discipline-specific application. The guide stresses that purchasing institution-wide tool licences achieves little without parallel investment in faculty development, course redesign, and leadership coordination across the whole institution.

*For educators:* If AI literacy is now as foundational as writing or numeracy, how should institutions restructure faculty hiring, induction, and ongoing professional development so that every instructor — regardless of discipline — can teach it with genuine confidence?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/campus/ai-literacy-everyones-responsibility
