# AI in Education Digest — 25 May 2026

---

**[UK — Higher Education Policy]** — A Higher Education Policy Institute audit of all 163 UK institutions with degree-awarding powers found that 40% have no publicly accessible AI policy, while those that do consistently "use the language of education while operating as compliance instruments." Researcher Sam Illingworth at Edinburgh Napier University identified a systematic gap in which policies that claim to support critical thinking function mainly as audit trails and surveillance mechanisms rather than genuine guidance for students or staff.

*For educators:* If your institution's AI policy promises student support but operates primarily as a compliance document, who is actually being protected — and from what?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/news/university-ai-policies-promise-support-deliver-surveillance

---

**[UK — University Curriculum Redesign]** — The University of Surrey has completed a systematic redesign of every degree programme to embed discipline-specific AI use from September 2026, applying to current and incoming students alike and shifting assessment from outputs to process. Concrete examples include politics students interrogating ChatGPT's explanation of election results against established theories and real British Election Study data, and civil engineering students using AI to design a six-storey hotel before verifying every AI output against hand calculations.

*For educators:* When AI is embedded as a required disciplinary tool rather than an optional add-on, which of your existing assessments become meaningless — and what process-focused alternatives could better reveal authentic student understanding?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/news/surrey-redesigns-all-degrees-embed-discipline-specific-ai

---

**[Australia — Exam Integrity]** — Researchers at Deakin University warn that AI-enabled wearable devices — smart glasses, earbuds, rings, pendants, and hearing aids — are effectively undetectable in traditional invigilated exam settings, constituting a "second wave" of disruption following text-based generative AI. With 7 million pairs of Ray-Ban Meta AI glasses already sold globally, the researchers argue that institutions must redesign assessment proactively rather than wait until wearables are as ubiquitous as smartphones.

*For educators:* If you can no longer reliably verify that a student completed an exam without AI assistance, what does "demonstrating mastery" mean in your discipline — and how would you redesign high-stakes assessment to evaluate competence rather than unaided recall?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/news/no-defence-against-wearable-ai-exams-researchers-warn
