**Dominican Republic / Caribbean Higher Education** — An educator at Universidad del Caribe (UNICARIBE) argues that traditional teacher-directed instruction — where the teacher exclusively holds and transmits knowledge while students play a passive role — is no longer fit for purpose in the generative-AI era. The article calls for repositioning GenAI as a catalyst for moving education beyond rote memorisation toward curiosity, depth and critical inquiry, with the future of teaching belonging to those who help students think, question and learn *alongside* AI rather than despite it.

*For educators:* If AI can now handle the transmission of factual content, what is the most irreplaceable thing you still do in a classroom — and are you spending most of your time on it?

**UK / Creative Higher Education** — Alan Beattie, head of education in De Montfort University's School of Design Innovation and programme leader for Fashion Textile Design, describes how his students are now required to document every AI tool they use and every prompt they write within submitted work, making visible exactly how much of the output is theirs. The approach treats generative AI as one instrument in a broader creative toolbox — no different in principle from a pencil or paintbrush — while directly tackling the homogenisation risk that arises when students adopt AI uncritically, and arguing that effective integration demands institution-wide backing rather than individual faculty decisions.

*For educators:* If every student had to submit a visible audit trail of their AI interactions alongside their final work, how would that change what you are actually assessing — and would it make the assessment more or less meaningful?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/campus/ai-use-tailored-creativeindustry-programmes-requires-institutionwide-support

**USA / Higher Education** — Virginia Tech is building an interdisciplinary AI minor that draws together students from computer science, engineering, art, business and the social sciences, on the premise that AI will reshape nearly every discipline and cannot be left to a single department. The minor combines technical foundations in how AI systems work with professional application, ethical decision-making and cross-disciplinary collaboration, aiming to produce graduates who can evaluate and deploy AI responsibly in context rather than in isolation.

*For educators:* When AI literacy is treated as a cross-disciplinary responsibility rather than a computer-science elective, which department in your institution is best placed to lead it — and which partnerships would need to be built first?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/campus/framework-ensuring-student-ai-proficiency
