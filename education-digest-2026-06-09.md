**[UK — Postgraduate Higher Education]** The London School of Innovation (LSI) has become the first UK institution to receive degree-awarding powers for an entirely AI-taught model: AI "private tutors" lead personalised instruction while human academics serve as a three-layer safety net (module leaders, a student-success team, and personal tutors) rather than as primary deliverers of teaching. LSI plans to welcome its first master's students this month, charging over £10,000 for online programmes in machine learning, digital innovation, and business transformation.

*For educators:* If AI can handle primary instruction at master's level well enough to satisfy a national regulator, what now constitutes the irreplaceable, accreditable contribution of a human lecturer?

**[UK — Universities / International Students]** A YouGov survey of 2,373 UK university students found that three-quarters of those who use AI feel stressed their work will be wrongly flagged as cheating by automated detection tools, with international students twice as likely to report experiencing "a lot" of stress compared with domestic peers. Researchers urge universities to reconsider detection tools that routinely produce false positives, noting that over half of all students polled named "being accused of cheating when I did nothing wrong" as a major source of anxiety.

*For educators:* When AI detection tools create disproportionate harm for international students — who already navigate language-based disadvantages — whose responsibility is it to close that fairness gap before the tools are deployed?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/news/fear-being-flagged-ai-detectors-drives-stress-among-students

**[Pan-European — Doctoral Education]** A survey of 217 universities by the European University Association's Council for Doctoral Education found that more than two-thirds of European institutions report their PhD students using AI in the course of their research, with doctoral education leaders overall seeing "more opportunity than challenges" in the trend. The findings coincide with widespread calls to reform how researchers are assessed, signalling that AI adoption in the doctorate is already outpacing evaluation frameworks across both anglophone and non-anglophone research systems.

*For educators:* With two-thirds of European doctoral programmes already reporting widespread AI use, are supervisors equipped to distinguish AI-assisted intellectual breakthroughs from AI-substituted thinking — and should the assessment of doctoral candidates reflect that distinction?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/news/two-thirds-universities-report-ai-use-among-doctoral-students
