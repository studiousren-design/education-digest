# AI in Education Digest — 2026-06-25

---

**International Schools (Middle East / Southeast Asia)** — A UK-trained teacher with no programming background used vibe coding—generating software from plain-language prompts—to build an AI reading-recommendation app that ingests a school's library catalog, profiles each student's reading level and interests, and surfaces books most likely to excite them. What took weeks to prototype in late 2025 can now be done in hours, and the tool has already shifted books from untouched shelves into students' hands across international schools in the region.
*For educators:* If a single teacher can build a personalised AI tool in hours without coding skills, what does that imply about which professional-development capabilities schools should now be prioritising?
Source: https://www.edsurge.com/news/vibe-coding-sparked-reading-interest-in-my-students

**United Kingdom** — UK IT body Jisc and the Environmental Association for Universities and Colleges (EAUC) have published a sustainability guide urging university and college leaders to collectively demand that AI vendors disclose per-query energy and water consumption data. Because most providers do not publish usage-level emissions figures, institutions currently have no way to incorporate AI use into their carbon accounting—and the guide warns that unchecked AI adoption could undermine net-zero commitments.
*For educators:* As campuses scale up AI tools for teaching and research, who should be responsible for tracking—and disclosing—their environmental cost: the institution, the vendor, or both?
Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/news/universities-should-demand-emissions-data-ai-firms

**Indonesia** — At a Jakarta forum of South-east Asian educators, Indonesia's deputy minister for higher education Stella Christie argued that "upskilling" is the wrong framework for the AI era because it implies workers' existing capabilities have become obsolete. She proposed "up-systeming" instead—redesigning institutional ecosystems to cultivate and better match the skills populations already have, rather than treating people as deficient products to be retrained.
*For educators:* Does framing AI-era professional development as "upskilling" inadvertently signal to teachers that their expertise is now outdated, and how might a systems-change mindset reshape the way institutions approach AI integration?
Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/news/changing-system-more-important-upskilling
