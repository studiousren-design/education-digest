# Education & AI Digest — 15 June 2026

---

**[UK / Higher Education]** — New research reported in Times Higher Education shows that Turnitin's AI-detection software systematically miscalculates how much LLM-generated content appears in student essays: it inflates scores for work that is 15–40% AI-written while consistently under-flagging essays that are 70–100% AI-generated. The findings undermine the tool's reliability as an evidence base for misconduct rulings and prompt researchers to recommend that institutions shift focus from detection to assessment redesign.

*For educators:* If the most widely used AI detection tool cannot reliably distinguish between occasional and heavy AI use, is it ethically defensible to base academic misconduct rulings on its scores?

**[USA / K–12 Schools]** — The latest CoSN State of Ed Tech report finds that three-quarters of US school districts now have AI guidelines in place — up sharply from 57% a year ago — yet a parallel Gallup/Walton Family Foundation survey reveals that the vast majority of teachers have still not received formal guidance on how to use AI, with roughly one-third receiving none at all. The data expose a widening gap between fast-moving district policy and the on-the-ground readiness of the educators expected to implement it responsibly.

*For educators:* As district AI policies multiply faster than teacher training programmes can keep pace, who bears responsibility for ensuring frontline educators are not left to navigate high-stakes decisions about these tools alone?

Source: https://www.edsurge.com/news/ai-is-in-schools-teachers-are-not-ready

**[International / Higher Education]** — In a wide-ranging reflection marking four years since ChatGPT's launch, five scholars from across the world told Times Higher Education how generative AI has reshaped their academic practice — from hallucinated citations infiltrating scientific literature to an AI scoring 95% on an undergraduate quantum physics exam when fed papers verbatim. OpenAI itself has responded by building a tool specifically designed to discourage students from over-relying on AI-generated answers, a signal that even the technology's creators view unchecked dependence as a pedagogical problem.

*For educators:* Four years into the generative-AI era, which assumptions about student learning and academic integrity have proved most durable — and which have had to be quietly abandoned?

Source: https://www.timeshighereducation.com/depth/are-academics-making-em-dash-ai
